We have collaborated with two nonprofits to offer you projects that will both impact the New Haven community, and be used by many people. Nonprofit Projects

MakeHaven
Mission Our mission is to help people unlock their innovative potential. We provide dozens of tools to the New Haven community for them to build and develop projects for their communities or for themselves. In addition, we host several workshops that range from simple clothing repair, to 3D modeling, and all the way to creating a business from a prototype.
Project New Member Onboarding Slackbot
Description MakeHaven wants to automate the following processes through a Slackbot:
  1. Provide a link to an online waver (you can use a link to MakeHaven as a placeholder).
  2. Watch orientation videos (found on YouTube, you can put your favorite videos as placeholders).
  3. Sign up for Slack Community
  4. Schedule key pickup
  5. Identify area of interest (3D printing, laser cutting, woodworking).
  6. Watch tool orientation video (also found on YouTube).
  7. Schedule meeting with volunteer coordinator for tool to be approved for use.
Once the new member has been approved to use the tool, the Slackbot should remind the member about other tools available, and about their respective orientations. If possible, after 30, and 60 days, the Slackbot could check in if everything is okay, and suggest orientations for other tools.
This description is very broad on purpose. There will be people who will answer any questions you may have at the hackathon, but remember that this will actually be used and modified by people. Therefore, try to make it as versatile, general, and easy-to-use as possible.
Suggested APIs The Slack API
Environment for Deployment It will be deployed on a service like Heroku.
Extra Functionalities for Extra Help
  • Have the bot suggest attendance to upcoming events using data from Google Calendar or Evenbrite.
  • Suggest a meeting time through Google calendar for step 7 of the orientation.

NH/LSCP
Mission The New Haven/León Sister City Project is a binational organization promoting social justice, education and sustainable development in the New Haven and León communities.
The primary work of the NH/LSCP in León, Nicaragua is to support community based initiatives in the rural communities of Goyena and Troilo and to facilitate programs and projects that improve public health and community based education, support women’s rights, and address root causes of poverty.
In New Haven, we work to respond to climate change. In both communities we work to build local leadership and capacity and address root causes of poverty and injustice.
Project A carbon fund calculator available as a (preferably) WordPress plugin or an embeddable app. The purpose of this calculator is to both fundraise money, and raise awareness about the impact of greenhouse gasses.
Description This project is based on the Yale Community Carbon Fund calculator available here. The methodology of the calculator is here. There is a LOT that can be added and modified!
Suggested APIs This RESTful HTTP interface lets you calculate carbon emissions easily, and this site contains several APIs to help you get started.
Extra Functionalities for Extra Help The plugin could use a generic wrapper that would allow it to be incorporated on different sites besides those created with WordPress. It would also be really helpful if the plugin had a placeholder for a PayPal button so a WordPress admin could integrate the donation calculated with the WordPress component for collecting a donation.

Example Projects


Peace Corps: Malaria Prevention App
This project consists of an application designed to help Peace Corps Volunteers to sustain life-saving malaria preventio tactics over their time of service. For more details, look at their GitHub repo.
Systers: PowerUp
PowerUp is an educational choose-your-own-adventure game that utilizes a users uploaded curriculum to empower pre-adolescents to take charge of their reproductive health. For more details, look at their GitHub repo.
SATrek: EarthTrek
SATrek was one of the teams that participated in NASA's Space Apps challenge, where people from around the world had 48 hours to come up with a great solution to one of their challenges. This team developed an interactive 3D application that encourages the use of data that NASA's EOS program collects through its satellites, and understand its importance. For more information, look here
Lemon Py: Pollen Alert
Lemon Py was one of the winners of the NASA's Space Apps challenge. This team developed an app that shows the distribution of panel in the city of Buenos Aires, and guides people through the path with least pollen to minimize allergic reactions. For more information, look here.

About

Why a Civic Track

In Yale Code4Good, we believe that there is a huge gap between technology and social good. Through this track, we hope to encourage students to think about technology as a way to innovate and improve our society. Therefore, we strongly encourage you to work on the nonprofit projects. Your project will have a real impact, and you can talk about it to employers ;D. If the projects are not your cup of tea, but you still want to be part of this track, awesome! You have now joined the movement to make our society better through tecnology.
If you have any questions, send us an email at code4good at yale dot edu
